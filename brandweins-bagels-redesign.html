Our Story

"There's something magical about a bagel — it can put a smile on your face."
— Alex Brandwein, Founder

A New York native, Alex spent eight years in investment banking and private equity before enrolling at UNC's Kenan-Flagler Business School. When he got to Chapel Hill, he loved everything about it except one thing: there was nowhere downtown to grab a real bagel. For Alex, it was never really just about the bagel — it's about treating people right and taking the time to actually get to know them, and bagels became the vehicle to make that happen. Brandwein's opened its doors on Rosemary Street in August 2020, and has been boiling and baking the New York way ever since.

Our Mission

Building community, one day at a time.

Mission

"Our mission is to make people's days a little bit better with good food done well."

That mission has always started with Chapel Hill. When we opened our doors on Rosemary Street, the goal was never just to sell bagels — it was to plant roots in this town and become a real part of its everyday rhythm. Every bagel we boil and bake is for a neighbor, a student, a coworker on their way into work — someone we want to actually know by name. That promise lives in the details: organic, non-GMO flour from Lindley Mills in Graham, NC, real barley malt syrup, and the same boil-then-bake method New York bagels have used for a hundred years — because this community deserves it done right, morning after morning.

Vision

A Chapel Hill where neighbors know each other a little better.

Our vision isn't about how big we get — it's about how close we get. We want Brandwein's to be the kind of place where students, professors, families, and neighbors who'd otherwise never cross paths end up at the same counter, trading stories over the same bagels. Where a quick stop turns into a familiar face, and a familiar face turns into a friend. We want to be woven into this town the way Chapel Hill has been woven into us — a place people build their mornings, their traditions, and their memories around for years to come.

How It's Made

Water first. Heat second.

Scroll through the same three steps that turn a ring of dough into a real New York bagel — the process that's made it famous for a hundred years.

Step 01

Rolled & proofed

01 — ROLLED & PROOFED

It starts with local flour

Dough made with organic, non-GMO Lindley Mills flour from Graham, NC is hand-rolled into rings and left to proof overnight, building the flavor a shortcut bagel skips entirely.

02 — BOILED IN THE KETTLE

A quick dip, and everything changes

Every ring gets boiled for a few minutes before baking. "This extra step is what makes the outside thin, crispy and glossy while the inside stays light and airy," Alex says.

03 — BAKED TILL GOLDEN

Straight from kettle to oven

Baked until deep golden-brown outside and pillowy soft within — ready for a shmear, a stack of lox, or a fully built deli sandwich.
